Tokenizing Corporate Credentials

Facilitating authentication, KYC due diligence, and ongoing risk assessment

The Problem

The KYC process requires banks to collect and assess updated identity-related documentation (credentials) from all their clients based on a largely manual, repetitive, inefficient, time-consuming, cumbersome, non-standardised, and expensive process that lacks in security. Banks alone spend over $500m on KYC annually.

Built on DLT

Umazi is built on distributed ledger technology, allowing participants to agree on a shared source of truth and perception using execution of smart contracts over secure, immutable data. The value and security of a network built on DLT grows exponentially with the number of participants. Existing systems can also be integrated easily and solutions can be tailored to meet specific user and client requirements.

Enterprise Ready

Umazi represents a utility to all participants but is built from the client's perspective and provides enterprise level integration into financial institutions and corporate entities as well as legal and data verification services. Clients are able to control and manage their own data, platform and client relationships, whilst allowing financial institutions to manage risk assessment and compliance.

Built for Interoperability

Umazi is built on an open data model which provides interoperability and scalability of data being shared on a distributed ledger. By building on a network that is owned by participants and not by a single trusted party, participants are able to build their own solutions and integrations utilising the same underlying data, therefore incentivising participants to utilise the Umazi utility.

Credentials as an Asset

Umazi allows identity credentials to be stored in a utility asset which can be distributed to members of the Umazi network on a need-to-know basis to faciliate KYC, due diligence and ongoing risk assessment.

Verifiable Credentials

Credentials such as passports, plastic cards, driving licenses and qualifications can be digitized, stored privately in the Umazi network and verified by 3rd parties such as local authorities and governing bodies.

Risk Profiling

The Umazi network allows subscribers to utility assets to obtain up-to-date claims and credentials to better profile and assess customer risk levels, allowing businesses to take strategic action in real time.

Self-Sovereign Identity

Umazi allows network members to take ownership of their identity information by creating self-issued claims which contain atomic units of information such as company name, number and registered status.

Automated Verification

The Umazi network leverages an event driven model where state transitions on the distributed ledger raise events which can trigger processes within integrated systems to provide automation over digital claims and data representing legal entity identification.

Pandemic Proof

The Umazi network provides the ability for identity information to be established and attested so that remote onboarding of new customers can be performed in lockdown, just as easily as if you were in the office.

Claims Model

The claims provides a secure, digital solution to sharing attestable facts to establish existence and uniqueness of atomic units of information. Claims also have the ability to allow data to become interoperable and consistent across software platforms.

Time and Cost Saving

Process automation and digital smart contracts allow claims, assets and attestations to be created, amended, revoked and distributed across the network without requiring manual intervention, raising the bar for error tolerance and allows the network to scale effectively, saving time and money.
